First Aid


Heimlich Maneuver

This is administered to people who have a blocked airway. Some signs are not coughing, unable to speak or breathe, or waving and gesturing to their throat

1. Get the person to stand up.

2. Position yourself behind the person.

3. Lean the person forward and give five blows to their back with the heel of your hand.

4. Place your arms around their waist.

5. Make a fist and place it just above the navel, thumb side in.

6. Grab the fist with your other hand and push it inward and upward at the same time. Perform five of these abdominal thrusts.

7. Repeat until the object is expelled and the person can breathe or cough on their own.

For Yourself

1. Make a fist and place it just above your navel, thumb side in.

2. Grab the fist with your other hand and push it inward and upward at the same time. Perform five of these abdominal thrusts.

3. Repeat until the object is expelled and you can breathe or cough on your own.

For more information, visit https://www.healthline.com/health/heimlich-maneuver#steps.

CPR

Before Providing CPR:

1. Ensure the scene is safe and that the person is in need of CPR

2. Ask a bystander to call 911 and get an AED

3. Open the personâ€™s airway by tilting their head back and check for breathing. If no breaths are evident begin CPR

Red Cross CPR:

1. Deliver 2-inch deep compressions at 100 beats per minute

2. Deliver 2 rescue breaths by tilting their head back, pinching their nose, and creating a mouth to mouth seal.

3. Continue CPR steps till a trained medical professional arrives at the scene.

Visit https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/cpr/performing-cpr/cpr-steps for more information.

Basic First Aid for Bleeding:

Cover the wound with gauze or cloth and apply direct pressure. Add more layers till the blood flow stops.

Basic First Aid for Burns:

1. Flush the burned area with cool running water

2. Apply a light gauze bandage

3. Take pain relief medication if necessary

4. Do not break formed blisters

Basic First Aid for Sprains:

1. Immobilize the limb

2. Apply a cold pack

3. Elevate it

4. See Doctor for further diagnosis and treatment

Basic First Aid for Bee Stings:

1. Take out the stinger

2. If the person is allergic to bee stings, administer an EpiPen

3. Apply a cold pack

4. Use an antihistamine like Benadryl (diphenhydramine) to reduce swelling and itching.

5. Take pain relief medication

All of this was sourced at: https://www.verywellhealth.com/basic-first-aid-procedures-1298578.
